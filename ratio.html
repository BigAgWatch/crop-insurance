Crop Insurance Indemnities for Corn

1989

The above map shows the loss ratio for corn crop insurance from 1989 to 2015.

The “Loss ratio” compares the amount of insurance premiums paid into policies to the amount of insurance indemnities – or payouts – later paid back out.

A loss ratio below 1.0 means that insurance premiums totaled more than insurance indemnities. A loss ratio greater than 1.0 means that claims paid out totaled more than premiums.

For example, during the 2012 drought, loss ratios in Illinois reached as high as 16.6. Use the slide bar located under the map to change from year to year.

Use your mouse to scroll over a specific county and see exact loss ratios. Lower loss ratios are shown in light green, while higher loss ratios are shown in red.

Data: U.S. Department of Agriculture, Risk Management Agency, Summary of Business Reports as of February 24, 2016.
